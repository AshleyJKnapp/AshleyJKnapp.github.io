About Me

Hi, I'm Ash!

I'm a game developer and artist from Southern California, as well as a recent graduate from UC Santa Cruz. I aspire to create experiences that emotionally impact people in the same way I have been shaped by media. As I am both educated in programming and do art as a hobby, I enjoy contributing to both of those aspects in game development and understand how to bridge the gap between the two. And, although less experienced, I also have an interest in music creation and 3D modeling, which helps me bridge gaps to those as well!

In my smaller projects, I've found that I tend to gravitate towards gameplay design and visual design, but I've found myself to be a generalist for the most part, as I typically enjoy working on a variety of tasks to help move the project forward. I love learning new skills, so I'm always willing to learn new systems, game engines, and languages for myself and the projects I work on. I also tend to help my teams out with debugging as I am skilled at identifying issues and am determined to find solutions.

Some more about me!

Maybe this is a little out of the norm, but I want to talk deeper about some things I care for a lot, so that you get to know who I really am and just how important creating experiences is to me! This next bit is about my passions and motivations to create, so if you just wanted to know what I can do, you've seen it all and can safely stop reading here.

One of my favorite parts of the development process is seeing all the pieces come together to create something playable. I enjoy seeing and hearing about player's reactions to the game I helped create. I love designing interactive experiences to provoke a specific emotional response, and it is very satisfying when that feeling lands!

I am someone who values authenticity and uniqueness, and when it comes to creating, I greatly value unique and original ideas. A creative community that inspires me deeply is Vocaloid, a music scene built around vocal synthesis software. I am very influenced by how aspiring creators using vocal synths get to share their message with the world. Seeing so many unique stories told by so many ordinary people through music is just one thing that has kept me so inspired to continue creating in my life and has taught me the importance of self-expression. I hold basically the feelings when it comes to the indie game dev scene as well.

Unique experiences created by people are very important to me. As I mentioned earlier, I want to make things that impact people in the way I have been impacted! I want players to be left with an incredibly real feeling caused completely by a digital world. It could be from the awe of worldbuilding, or the hope or sadness from a story, or the thrill of playing something that keeps them engaged and on their toes while they learn and become more skilled. I think games are beautiful, and are not only an art form, but a grand culmination of numerous art forms.
